02 — Color Families

How the palette breaks down

Blues and teals command 43% of all color/stone purchases — more than the next three families combined. Whites and neutrals (Pearl, Mother of Pearl, Clear, Snow) are primarily a holiday story, while greens and pinks have been building through spring.

03 — Trend Over Time

Seasonal shifts in color preference

Two clear spring signals: Greens doubled their share from ~8% in winter to 16% by March, and Pinks/Purples nearly tripled from 5% at the holiday peak to 12% by April. Dark/Earthy stones (Black Onyx, Tigers Eye) were at their strongest in October–November and have softened since. Blues/Teals held a consistently dominant position throughout.

05 — By Age Group

Color preferences across generations

Blues and teals grow as a share of purchases with age — from 43% of color choices for the 19–22 group to nearly 50% among 70–79-year-olds. Greens and dark earthy stones have a mid-life peak (30–39), while whites and neutrals skew notably younger. Reds and corals show a gradual increase with age.

06 — By State

Geographic color preferences

Florida stands out with the highest Blues/Teals share of any state (48%), likely reflecting coastal lifestyle associations. Arkansas leans most toward dark and classic tones. Virginia and Texas show the strongest relative preference for Greens. The Southeast is broadly consistent, but meaningful differences exist at the margin.

08 — Catalog Coverage

What's offered vs. what's selling

Matched the 180-day sales data against the full product catalog (1,455 SKUs) to understand how many styles each color is offered in, and how hard each color is working per SKU. A high units-per-SKU score means strong demand concentrated in fewer styles — a signal that a color could support broader rollout. A low score flags styles that may be over-represented in the catalog relative to customer demand.

Notes on methodology. Gold, Silver, Post Back, and chain-type variants were excluded from this analysis to surface true color and stone preferences. "Post Back" appears as an earring hardware attribute and not a color choice. Letter variants (A–N) representing charm/initial identifiers were also excluded. The catalog join matched ~96% of revenue using normalized SKU matching (stripping color prefixes and chain-length suffixes). The age analysis is based on post-purchase survey responses matched to ~10,100 orders (~28% coverage); the 60–79 cohort has the strongest signal. April 2026 covers only the first 15 days.
