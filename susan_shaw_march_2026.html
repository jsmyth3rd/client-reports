Geography note: The West region peaks at 5 PM Eastern (2 PM Pacific), aligning with a lunchtime browse for West Coast customers. The South peaks at 9–10 AM. The Northeast peaks at 1 PM. This pattern is more geographic than age-driven — older age groups (60–79) peak at 9–10 AM consistent with the brand's Southern core customer, while younger buyers (30–49) skew afternoon and evening.
Section 07

Returns Analysis

Return behavior by order quantity. Do customers who buy more pieces return more?

Return Rate by Items Purchased per Order
% of orders associated with a return event · by quantity group
Key finding: Return rates are very low across all quantity groups (under 2%). There is no meaningful pattern suggesting that customers who buy more pieces return more — the highest return rate belongs to the 6+ item group at 1.96%, but this group represents only 51 orders. The 2-item group (the largest segment at 2,879 orders) has the lowest return rate at 0.14%. Overall, returns are not a concern tied to order size.

Section 08

Customer Survey

Post-purchase survey data via Grapevine. 1,457 responses across 4,791 orders (~30% response rate). Age question answered by all respondents; trend question answered by 617.

Customer Age Distribution
Survey respondents · 1,457 total
Style & Trend Requests
What customers wish we were doing more of · 617 responses
Age insight: The 60–69 and 70–79 brackets account for 57% of all survey respondents, anchoring the core customer profile. Customers under 40 represent only 6% of respondents. The brand's core is firmly 50–79.
Product request insight: Rings are the single most-requested category (86 mentions) yet represent only 2.7% of net sales — a gap worth noting for future product development. Earrings and bracelets also rank high in requests, consistent with their current product share. Crosses/religious themes (34 mentions) and Pearls (28) appear consistently, especially among the 60–79 age cohort.
